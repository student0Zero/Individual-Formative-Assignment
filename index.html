Mindfulness can be described as being aware of your actions and your surroundings. It's about being present in the moment and taking note of what's going on around you. Being mindful is about noticing the distant sound of a car, or the weight of the keys under your finger tips as you type. It's about being aware of what you are doing and not being overly reactive or overwhelmed by what's going on around you.

Spirituality and mindfulness have been intertwined throughout human history. Many religions make use of mindfulness practices and have done so for thousands of years. Mindfulness has been associated with Buddhism, Hinduism, Judaism, Christianity and Islam.

Distractions are everywhere. Have you ever found yourself scrolling mindlessly on your phone, while watching Netflix?

With all these modern distractions, redirecting your focus and training your mind so that you can focus on a single task is sometimes needed.

Mindfulness training can be a handy strategy to use with clients who mindlessly eat at their desks at work, or in the evenings while watching YouTube videos.
